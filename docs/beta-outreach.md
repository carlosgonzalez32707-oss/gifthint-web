## Template E — Reddit Post (r/gifts or r/GiftIdeas)

**Title:** I built a Chrome extension that lets you save gifts from any store and share a wishlist — looking for honest beta feedback

---

Hey everyone,

I built something that I think fits this community and I'd love some real feedback from people who actually think about gifts.

**The problem I was trying to solve:**
Every year someone asks what I want for my birthday and I send a mix of screenshots, Amazon links, and "honestly anything from here" — and it's chaos. Universal wishlists exist but they're all clunky or locked to one store.

**What GiftHint does:**
It's a Chrome extension with a floating heart button that appears on product pages. Click it, and the item (name, price, image, link) gets saved to your personal list at gifthint.com/list/[yourname]. You share that one link. Gifters can browse it without creating an account, and they can claim items they're buying so nothing gets bought twice.

Works on Amazon, Etsy, ASOS, Apple, and most major retailers — basically anywhere with a product page.

**What I'm looking for feedback on:**
- Did the heart button appear when you browsed a product page? (Any sites where it didn't show up?)
- Did the public list page load correctly and make sense to someone seeing it for the first time?
- Is the claim flow intuitive, or confusing?
- Anything that felt broken, slow, or annoying?

**Example list (no account needed to view):** [YOUR_LIST_URL]

Brutal honesty welcome — I'd rather fix things now than after a full launch.
